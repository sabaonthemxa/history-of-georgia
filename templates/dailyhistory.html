Daily History — Ancient Georgia

Day 1: Foundation of Iberia

In the 4th century BC, the Kingdom of Iberia (Kartli) began to unify the eastern Georgian tribes under a centralized monarchy. King Pharnavaz I is credited with establishing the first royal dynasty, creating governmental positions, and instituting Georgian coinage.

Day 2: Colchis and Trade

The western kingdom of Colchis was renowned for its wealth and trade in metals and gold. Greek traders from the Black Sea visited Colchis, and it became famous in mythology as the destination of Jason and the Argonauts seeking the Golden Fleece.

Day 3: Early Conflicts

During the 3rd century BC, Iberia and Colchis faced invasions from neighboring tribes and empires, including the Persians and local Caucasian tribes. These conflicts helped shape the political and military structures of early Georgian kingdoms.
