farnavaz I

Farnavaz I was the first king of ancient Iberia (Kartli) who ruled around the 3rd century BC. He unified the tribes of eastern Georgia, established the first centralized Georgian state, and created important government positions like the spaspeti (military commander). He strengthened the kingdom through alliances.

Georgian History — A Long Overview

Georgia sits at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, and its history is a long story of kingdoms and empires, faith and culture, resilience and renewal. Below is an extended, continuous narrative that traces the main phases of Georgian history from antiquity to the modern era.

The lands of what we now call Georgia were inhabited from prehistoric times and gave rise to early kingdoms in antiquity. On the western shore of the Black Sea lay Colchis, famous in Greek myth as the destination of Jason and the Argonauts and as a center of early metallurgy and trade. Inland, in the eastern highlands, the kingdom of Iberia (Kartli) emerged. Between these polities local tribal groups and city-states developed distinct languages, art, and political institutions that would slowly coalesce into larger states.

From the classical era onward Georgia’s strategic position made it a contested borderland. Successive great powers — Achaemenid Persia, Hellenistic kingdoms, and later Rome and Byzantium — influenced local elites, religion, and administration. Yet Georgian rulers preserved autonomy at times and absorbed foreign ideas into native traditions.

One of the watershed moments in Georgian history was the adoption of Christianity in the early fourth century. Around the year 327, the kingdom of Iberia accepted Christianity as a state religion under King Mirian III and the missionary efforts of figures like Saint Nino. This conversion reshaped Georgian identity: the Georgian Orthodox Church became a pillar of cultural unity, literacy, and artistic production, commissioning churches, frescoes, and manuscript traditions that survive to this day.

Georgia’s history is not a single straight line but a tapestry of kingdoms, faith, and resilience—an island of distinct culture at the crossroads of empires.

Short timeline (high-level):

  • Antiquity: Colchis and Iberia (early kingdoms and Greek interactions)
  • 4th century: Christianization of Iberia
  • 9th–13th centuries: Bagratid unification and Golden Age under David IV and Queen Tamar
  • 13th–18th centuries: Mongol invasions, Ottoman and Persian contests, fragmentation
  • 19th century: Russian annexation and national revival
  • 1918–1921: First modern independence (short-lived)
  • 1921–1991: Soviet period
  • 1991: Independence restored; 2003: Rose Revolution and reforms
